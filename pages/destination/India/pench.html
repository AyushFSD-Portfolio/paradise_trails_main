Why Visit Pench?

📚 Jungle Book's Birthplace

Pench is the legendary setting that inspired Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book." The story of Mowgli began here in 1831 when a wolf-child was discovered in these very forests. Walk in the footsteps of Mowgli, Baloo, and Shere Khan in their original homeland.

🐅 Tiger Paradise

Home to over 50 tigers across its 758 sq km area, Pench offers excellent tiger sighting opportunities. The park's open grasslands and mixed deciduous forests provide perfect habitat for these magnificent predators and their prey species.

🌿 Rich Wildlife Diversity

Discover 39 species of mammals, 210+ bird species, and 50+ butterfly species. Spot leopards, sloth bears, Indian wolves, wild dogs, gaur, sambars, spotted deer, and langurs in their natural habitat across varied ecosystems.

🏞️ Scenic River Landscape

The meandering Pench River flows through the heart of the park, creating stunning landscapes and providing water sources for wildlife. The riverine forests and grasslands offer spectacular photography opportunities year-round.

🚙 Multiple Safari Zones

Explore six distinct safari zones - Turia, Karmajhiri, Jamtara, Telia, Rukhad, and Khawasa. Each zone offers unique terrain and wildlife experiences, from dense forests to open grasslands and river valleys.

🦉 Night Safari Adventures

Experience unique night safaris in the buffer zones (Rukhad & Khawasa) where you can spot nocturnal wildlife including leopards, wolves, jackals, and various nocturnal birds in their natural nighttime activities.

Location & Getting Here

Pench National Park is strategically located on the border of Madhya Pradesh and Maharashtra, in the Seoni and Chhindwara districts. This tiger reserve offers excellent connectivity while maintaining its pristine wilderness character as the original Jungle Book setting.
